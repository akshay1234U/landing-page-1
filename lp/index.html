Myopia (Nearsightedness)


Myopia occurs when the eye is too long relative to the focusing power of the cornea and lens.
This causes light rays to focus at a point in front of the retina rather than directly on its surface, resulting in blurred distance vision.spactacles have negative vision number


Hyperopia (Farsightedness)


Hyperopia occurs when the eye is too short relative to the focusing power of the cornea and lens, causing light rays to focus behind the retina.
This results in difficulty seeing objects up close, while distant objects may be seen more clearly.spactacles have positivity vision number


Presbyopia


Presbyopia occurs when the lens of the eye becomes less flexible, making it difficult to change its shape to focus on close objects.
This loss of flexibility results in blurred vision at close distances.upper lens have negative vision number and lower lens have positive vision number
